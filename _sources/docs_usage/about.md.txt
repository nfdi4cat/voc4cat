# About Voc4Cat

## Community

The vocabulary and tooling are a community effort. See the project contributors listing and the Zenodo record for citation metadata and contributor details:

- [Project contributors](https://github.com/nfdi4cat/voc4cat/graphs/contributors) on GitHub
- [Zenodo record](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.8313340) (includes authors and contributor metadata)

Maintenance and editorial decisions are handled by the [Voc4Cat curators](https://github.com/orgs/nfdi4cat/teams/voc4cat-curators) group.
For maintainer contact or editorial queries, please open an issue in the [Voc4Cat repository](https://github.com/nfdi4cat/voc4cat/issues).

## Citing Voc4Cat

If you use Voc4Cat in publications or software, please cite the deposited vocabulary either using the version-independent general citation

> Voc4Cat – A SKOS Vocabulary for the Catalysis Disciplines. nfdi4cat/voc4cat. Zenodo. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.8313340

or (preferred) the exact version that you used, e.g.

> Linke, D., Moustakas, N., Doerr, M., Schumann, J., Götte, M., Borgelt, H., Nentwich, M., Terboven, C., & Flecken, F. (2025). Voc4Cat – A SKOS Vocabulary for the Catalysis Disciplines, Release 2025-10-14 (v2025-10-14). Zenodo. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17351357

For the most recent citation, visit the [general Zenodo page](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.8313340).

## Guiding principles

Voc4Cat follows a set of guiding principles – openness of data, openness of code, and open infrastructure – to ensure the vocabulary remains discoverable, reusable and maintainable over time.

A recent paper refers to these underlying ideas as "O3" (open data, open code, open infrastructure). For more see [C. Hoyt and B. Gyori, 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-024-03406-w).
